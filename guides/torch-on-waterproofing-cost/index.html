Torch-On Waterproofing Cost, Cape Town 2026

Torch-on is the go-to system for flat and low-pitch concrete roofs in Cape Town: a bituminous membrane heat-welded to the surface in overlapping sheets. It's durable and long-lasting, but the quality of the install makes or breaks it. Here's what it costs in 2026 and how to compare quotes.

What the per-m² rate should include

A fair torch-on quote covers lifting and disposing of the old membrane, repairing cracks and blisters, priming the slab, the membrane itself, and proper detailing at upstands, parapets and outlets, finished with a reflective UV coating. If prep and detailing are 'extra', the cheap rate isn't cheap.

Why install quality matters more than brand

Torch-on fails at the joints and upstands, not in the middle of a sheet. Correct overlaps, fully welded seams and properly dressed upstands are what keep it watertight for 15 years. This is a job for an experienced specialist with the right gear, a poorly torched roof can leak within a season and is a genuine fire risk during install.

Torch-on vs acrylic

Torch-on costs more up front (R300, R500/m² vs R120, R250/m² for acrylic) but lasts far longer on a flat concrete roof and handles ponding water better. Acrylic is cheaper, more flexible and easier to re-coat, which suits balconies and parapets. For a main flat roof, torch-on is usually the better long-term value.

FAQs

How much does torch-on waterproofing cost per square metre?

In Cape Town in 2026, torch-on is roughly R300, R500/m² supplied and installed on a prepared concrete roof. A typical residential flat-roof job totals R8,000, R30,000+ depending on area, prep and access.

How long does torch-on waterproofing last?

A properly installed torch-on system lasts about 10 to 15 years, and reputable installers back it with a 5 to 10 year workmanship guarantee. Lifespan depends heavily on the quality of the seams and upstand detailing and on a maintained UV coating.
