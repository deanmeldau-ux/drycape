Rising Damp Treatment Prices, Cape Town 2026

Rising damp is the most over-diagnosed and most over-charged damp problem in Cape Town, so it pays to know the real numbers. Here are 2026 prices for treating rising damp properly, the new chemical damp-proof course and the all-important re-plastering.

Injection-only vs full treatment

The headline-cheap rising damp quote is usually injection only. But the salts already in the old plaster keep pulling moisture from the air, so the wall looks damp again within months even though the new barrier is working. The lasting fix removes the contaminated plaster and re-renders with a breathable, salt-resistant plaster, that's the price difference, and it's worth paying.

What drives the per-metre price

Linear metres of affected wall, the height the damp has risen (more wall to re-plaster), access and furniture, and whether external causes, high garden levels, leaking downpipes, also need fixing. A specialist quotes after measuring and metering the wall, not over the phone.

Watch for over-diagnosis

Genuine rising damp shows a clear tide-mark up to about a metre with salt deposits, on ground-floor walls. If your damp is high on the wall, in cold corners, or paired with window condensation, it may be condensation or penetrating damp, cheaper, different fixes. Don't pay for a DPC you don't need.

FAQs

How much does it cost to treat rising damp per metre in Cape Town?

Expect R350, R750 per linear metre for the chemical DPC injection alone, and R450, R1,100 per linear metre for the full treatment including salt-plaster removal and breathable re-plastering, the version that lasts.

Is it worth paying more for the full rising damp treatment?

Yes. Injection-only is the most common reason damp 'comes back': the barrier works but the salty old plaster keeps drawing moisture. Paying for the re-plaster and the external fixes is what turns a temporary patch into a 20-year fix backed by a real guarantee.
