Damp Proofing Cost in Cape Town, 2026 Price Guide

Damp proofing is one of those jobs where quotes vary wildly, mostly because 'damp' covers several different problems with different fixes. Here are the real 2026 ranges we see across Cape Town, what drives the price, and how to read a quote so you don't overpay or get caught by the cheap-then-it-fails trap.

Chemical damp-proof course (DPC) injectionR350, R750 / linear mDrilling and injecting a new water barrier at the base of the wall.

These are indicative Cape Town market ranges for 2026 to help you compare, not a quote.

Why damp quotes vary so much

The biggest variable is the type of damp. Rising damp (a new chemical DPC plus re-plastering) is the most labour-intensive and sits at the top of the range. Penetrating damp may only need an external repair and a sealing coat. Condensation-driven 'damp' may need ventilation rather than any damp-proofing at all. A quote that doesn't first say which type you have is a guess.

What you're actually paying for

A real damp-proofing price includes diagnosis (moisture meter, checking ground levels and drainage), the treatment itself, removal and disposal of salt-contaminated plaster, breathable salt-resistant re-plastering, and a written guarantee. The cheap quotes that 'come back' almost always skip the re-plaster and the cause.

How to avoid overpaying

Get two or three quotes for the same scope. Be wary of anyone who quotes over the phone without seeing the wall, who blames everything on rising damp (it's over-diagnosed), or who won't put a guarantee in writing. Conversely, the cheapest quote that only paints over the problem is the most expensive in the long run.

FAQs

Is rising damp expensive to fix in Cape Town?

A single room of rising damp typically runs R6,000, R15,000 fully treated, and a whole older home can reach R15,000, R30,000 or more. The cost is driven by linear metres of wall, the re-plastering, and access, not by brand of injection cream.

Why is one damp quote half the price of another?

Usually because it covers half the work, injection only, with no salt-plaster removal or re-plaster, or no fix for the external cause. That's the version that fails within a year or two. Compare scope, not just the number.
