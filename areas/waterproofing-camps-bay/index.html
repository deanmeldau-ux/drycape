What waterproofing involves

Waterproofing is the application of a barrier, a liquid membrane, torch-on bituminous sheet, cementitious slurry or specialised coating, that stops water penetrating a surface. On a Cape Town home it is most often applied to flat or low-pitch concrete roofs, parapet walls, box gutters, balconies, planter boxes, basements and retaining walls.

A proper job is never just 'a coat of paint'. The specialist first lifts and cleans the surface, repairs cracks and blisters, primes, then applies the membrane in layers, usually with a reinforcing fabric or fleece at joints and upstands where failures almost always start.

The right system depends on the surface and the exposure. Torch-on is the workhorse for flat concrete roofs; flexible acrylic and polyurethane liquid membranes suit balconies, parapets and detailed areas; bituminous and crystalline products are used below ground for basements and retaining walls.

Signs you need waterproofing in Camps Bay

  • Damp patches, bubbling paint or brown staining on ceilings after rain
  • Pooling water on a flat roof that doesn't drain away within a day
  • Cracked, brittle or lifting membrane on a flat roof or balcony
  • Water marks running down internal walls below a balcony or parapet
  • A musty smell in a basement, garage or ground-floor room in winter
  • Efflorescence (white salty deposits) on retaining or basement walls

Waterproofing in Camps Bay, FAQs

How long does waterproofing last?

A correctly installed torch-on system lasts roughly 10 to 15 years and most reputable specialists offer a 5 to 10 year workmanship guarantee. Liquid acrylic systems last around 5 to 8 years before a re-coat. Lifespan depends heavily on surface prep and on UV exposure, so the cheapest quote is rarely the longest-lasting.

Can waterproofing be done in winter?

Most systems need a dry surface and a rain-free window to cure, which is harder in a Cape winter. Urgent leaks are often stabilised with a temporary repair, then the full system is applied in the next dry spell. A good specialist will tell you honestly when conditions are wrong rather than coat a wet roof.

Do I need to waterproof a tiled pitched roof?

Usually no, a sound tiled pitched roof sheds water by design. Waterproofing is for flat and low-pitch concrete roofs, balconies, box gutters and below-ground walls. If a tiled roof leaks, you more likely need roof leak repair (cracked tiles, failed flashing or ridge).
